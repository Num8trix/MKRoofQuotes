FAQ

Roofing questions, answered straight

Updated 2026 · 4 min read

Short on time? Jump to a question below, or if you'd rather just talk it through, get a free quote and we'll answer everything on the survey visit.

Quotes & pricing

Is the quote actually free?

Yes. A roofer visits, assesses the job in person, and gives you a written price — no charge, no obligation to book.

How long does a quote take to arrive?

Most people hear back within 24 hours to arrange a survey. Genuine emergencies are prioritised same-day.

Will the final price match the quote?

It should. A proper quote is based on an in-person survey, not a guess over the phone, so the only thing that changes it is if you ask for extra work once we're on site.

Do you charge a call-out fee?

No call-out fee for a standard quote visit. Genuine emergency call-outs are priced as a fixed fee, agreed before anyone attends.

The work itself

How long does a re-roof take?

Most homes take 3–5 working days, weather permitting. Larger or more complex roofs can take longer — your quote will include a realistic timeframe.

Do I need to be home during the work?

Not for most of it, though it helps to be reachable in case anything unexpected turns up once tiles are off.

What happens to the old materials?

Old tiles, felt and waste are stripped and removed from site as part of the job — this should be confirmed in your written quote so there's no separate skip charge later.

Will you match my existing tiles?

For repairs and partial re-roofs, yes wherever possible — we'll look at colour, profile and age to get as close a match as we can.

Certification & guarantees

Are your roofers actually certified?

Yes — our roofing engineers are NFRC and CompetentRoofer registered and fully insured. Ask to see certification on the day if you'd like to check.

What guarantee do I get?

Materials and workmanship are both covered in writing before work starts, so you're not chasing a verbal promise afterwards.

What if something goes wrong after the job's finished?

Get in touch and we'll come back out under the guarantee — that's what it's there for.

Emergencies & insurance

What counts as an emergency?

An active leak, storm damage, or anything letting water into the house right now. These get a same-week response, often same-day.

Does home insurance cover the cost?

Sometimes — insurance typically covers sudden damage like a storm or fallen branch, but not gradual wear and tear. We can provide a report you can submit with a claim if the damage looks storm-related.

Didn't find your question? Ask us directly when you request a quote — no bots, an actual roofer will get back to you.

Emergency roof repair in Milton Keynes

Updated 2026 · 5 min read

Water coming through the ceiling doesn't wait for office hours, and neither do we. This page covers what to do right now if your roof is actively leaking or storm-damaged, what a same-day call-out actually involves, and what it costs.

Leaking right now? Request an emergency call-out and mark "Emergency — leaking now" on the form. We prioritise these above scheduled work.

What to do while you wait for a roofer

  • Contain the water. Place a bucket or bowl under the leak and move anything valuable out of the way.
  • Relieve a bulging ceiling. If plasterboard is bulging with trapped water, a small controlled hole in the centre (into a bucket) can stop it collapsing unpredictably.
  • Cut power to the area if water is near electrics. Switch off the relevant circuit at the consumer unit if you're able to safely.
  • Take photos. Useful for your roofer and for any home insurance claim, especially after a storm.
  • Don't go up on the roof yourself. A wet, storm-damaged roof is not the place for a DIY fix — leave it to someone with the right harness and ladder setup.

Common causes of emergency leaks

  • Slipped or missing tiles after high wind, exposing the underlay beneath.
  • Storm or fallen-branch damage that cracks tiles or punctures felt directly.
  • Failed flashing around chimneys, skylights or where a roof meets a wall.
  • Blocked or overflowing gutters forcing water back up under the roofline.
  • Perished flat roof felt or membrane, particularly after a hard frost followed by rain.

What a same-day call-out involves

An emergency visit is about stopping water getting in today, not necessarily the full permanent fix in one go:

  1. A roofer attends and identifies the source of the leak from the outside and, where needed, from the loft.
  2. Immediate temporary or permanent repair is made to stop water ingress — often re-securing or replacing tiles, or patching flashing.
  3. If further work is needed (e.g. a wider area of storm damage), you'll get a written quote for that on the spot.
  4. If the damage looks storm-related, we can provide a short report to support an insurance claim.

Common questions

How fast can someone actually get to me?

Genuine active leaks are typically attended same-day; storm damage assessments are usually within 24 hours.

Is a temporary fix enough, or will I need more work later?

Depends on the cause — a slipped tile is often a permanent fix in one visit, while wider storm damage may need a follow-up quote for a fuller repair.

Will this affect my insurance claim?

Getting the leak stopped quickly is usually the right call regardless — most insurers expect you to prevent further damage. Ask for a report at the time if you plan to claim.

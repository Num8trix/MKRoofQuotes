Common roof types in Bletchley

Bletchley's housing stock spans several eras, and roof jobs vary accordingly:

  • Older terraces and semis near the town centre — often original slate or aging concrete tile, sometimes with roofs approaching or past 40 years old.
  • 1960s–80s estates — typically concrete interlocking tile, the most common re-roof job we see locally.
  • Newer-build estates — generally younger roofs, more likely to need occasional repair (flashing, gutters) than a full replacement.
  • Garages and extensions — commonly flat roofs, felt or GRP, which tend to need attention sooner than the main pitched roof.

What we're most often called out for in Bletchley

  • Slipped or missing tiles after wind, particularly on older terraced rooftops.
  • Guttering and flashing repairs, often around chimneys on period properties.
  • Full re-roofs on 1960s–80s estate housing reaching the end of its original roof's life.
  • Flat roof repairs on garages and rear extensions.
